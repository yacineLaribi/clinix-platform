Expert Explanation

This patient presented with classic symptoms of an acute myocardial infarction: crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, shortness of breath, and nausea. The patient's risk factors (hypertension, diabetes, smoking, family history) all increase the likelihood of coronary artery disease.

The ECG findings of ST-segment elevation in the anterior leads (V1-V4) indicate an anterior wall STEMI, likely due to occlusion of the left anterior descending artery. The elevated cardiac biomarkers (Troponin I and CK-MB) confirm myocardial injury.

The appropriate treatment includes immediate reperfusion therapy (primary PCI preferred if available within 90 minutes, otherwise fibrinolytic therapy), antiplatelet therapy (aspirin, P2Y12 inhibitor), anticoagulation, and supportive care.
